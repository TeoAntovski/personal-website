ABOUT ME

Who I Am

Hello! I’m Teo Antovski, a passionate and motivated student from Skopje, Macedonia, currently pursuing a degree in Cyber Security at FINKI. I’ve always been fascinated by technology how it works, how it breaks, and how to protect it. My early interests in electronics and communication led me to graduate with excellence from SUGS Vlado Tasevski, where I specialized as a technician in electronics and telecommunications. That solid technical background laid the groundwork for my deeper dive into the digital world, where my focus has now shifted toward defending systems and information in an increasingly connected world.

What I Do

My journey has been hands-on and driven by real experience. I’ve completed internships at Setec in their Service Department, where I worked on diagnosing hardware failures, repairing electronic devices, and understanding the intricate relationship between software and hardware. At Baumer, I had the opportunity not just to learn, but to teach, helping others understand electrical circuits and get hands-on with CNC machinery. These experiences taught me how to troubleshoot, how to explain complex systems clearly, and how to stay calm under pressure, all skills that are deeply valuable in cyber security. Whether it’s analyzing vulnerabilities in code, securing a network, or just solving a tough problem, I bring persistence, curiosity, and a love for tech to everything I do.

What I Love

Outside the world of tech, I’m a person who thrives on creativity, movement, and challenge. I love to work out regularly, push my limits on hikes, get lost in great movies, and build things with my own two hands. One of my favorite personal projects was designing and constructing an entire summerhouse from scratch. I planned, budgeted, bought materials like wood and cement, and over the course of three months, brought it to life with my own tools and effort. It taught me patience, planning, and how good it feels to finish something big. I also love coffee especially Americanos. One cup in the morning and I'm fully locked in. It's more than a routine; it’s a ritual that fuels my focus, especially when coding or tackling a new project.
